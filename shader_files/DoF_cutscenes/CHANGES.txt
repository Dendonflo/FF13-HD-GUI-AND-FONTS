FF13-2 Cutscene DoF Shader Improvements
========================================


STEP 1 — Near blur gather (8b0d63a81922561b)
---------------------------------------------
Original: 4-tap cross filter (up/down/left/right, 1 texel apart).
          Very coarse blur, visible as a blocky square pattern on
          near-blurred objects.

Improved: 19-tap circular kernel — centre + ring of 6 + ring of 12.
          Produces round, smooth near blur instead of a cross shape.
          Kernel radius is driven by the render target texel size so
          it scales correctly at any resolution.


STEP 2 — Near blur spread / dilation (b32c3447f7a5418c)
---------------------------------------------------------
Original: Dilates the near CoC (blur radius) outward using the formula
          2 × max(blended, centre) − centre.
          This formula is correct in intent but was never clamped, so it
          could produce CoC values above 1.0 which fed into step 3.

Improved: Identical formula, identical tap positions and blend weights,
          but the result is wrapped in saturate() so CoC stays in [0, 1].
          This prevents the compositor from receiving out-of-range values
          that caused objects to appear over-blurred or smeared when they
          should have been sharp.


STEP 3 — Final compositor (67f7f77335ff7a82)
---------------------------------------------
Original: Used an additive blend (sharp + nearBlur × weight) which
          over-brightened the image and did not correctly fade between
          sharp and blurred layers. Far DoF was effectively disabled
          when near CoC was zero. Used 5 taps at 0.5–1.5 texels for
          the far blur — very narrow and resolution-dependent.

Improved: Replaced with two clean lerps:
            1. lerp(sharp, farBlur, farCoc)      — far field
            2. lerp(above, nearBlur, nearCoc)    — near field on top
          This means in-focus areas stay sharp, far objects fade into
          the far blur proportionally, and near objects fade into the
          near blur. Near blur correctly takes priority over far blur.

          Far blur kernel widened from 5 taps to 9 taps (centre + 8
          surrounding at 1.5 texels), giving a softer far field.

          Both CoC values are clamped with saturate() as an additional
          safety measure against any residual overflow from step 2.
